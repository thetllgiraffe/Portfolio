My Story

My origins in coding begin in highschool. During that time I would mess about with PC builds and would work on web games to understand how they worked. As the years went on I progressed further and further in game development. I obtained interests in other hobbies of course such as mountain biking, soccer, and tutoring. During the last year of highschool I helped trailblaze the beginnings of a web security extracurricular for those interested in cyber security.

Come college I started my undergraduate in biology. Coding became a side hobby but this is when I transitioned fully into web development. For most of my time there I was strictly a front end developer until I came across 100Devs. I began creating my own APIs and flushed out some of my favorite websites, such as Silken Strands. To this day I still remain active in the community and provide volunteer coding events for others to learn and help build out skills and portfolios such as Papa Don's Wings and Burgers.

Where can you find me today? Outside of the 100Devs community, you can find me out and about working with small and large businesses, providing opportunities to bring individuals of varying levels together, and if I'm not on the web, spending time with friends and family and enjoying nature. But if you'd like to join me in my adventures, feel free to reach out below.
